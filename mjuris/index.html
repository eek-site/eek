Dispute Resolution Process

Our Approach to Dispute Resolution

Eek Mechanical is committed to resolving disputes fairly and efficiently. We encourage open communication and seek mutually beneficial solutions wherever possible.

1

Initial Contact

Contact our customer service team on +64 9 872 4612. Most issues can be resolved at this level within 24-48 hours.

2

Formal Complaint

If unresolved, submit a formal complaint through our Customer Escalation Form. We'll acknowledge receipt and provide a full response within 30 days as per the Consumer Guarantees Act.

3

Investigation

Our legal team will investigate the matter, review relevant contracts and policies, and may contact you for additional information.

4

Resolution

We'll provide a written response with our findings and proposed resolution within 30 days of receiving your formal complaint, in accordance with the Consumer Guarantees Act.

5

External Options

If you're not satisfied with our resolution, you have several independent options available to you at no cost or low cost.

⚠️ Important Information

Customer Rights: Nothing in our dispute resolution process limits your rights under the Consumer Guarantees Act 1993 or Fair Trading Act 1986.

Free Process: Our internal dispute resolution process is free of charge to all customers.

Independent Advice: You may wish to seek independent legal advice. Citizens Advice Bureau provides free guidance: www.cab.org.nz

Emergency Services & Police

🚨 Emergency - 111

Call 111 when:

  • • Someone is badly injured or in danger
  • • There's a serious risk to life or property
  • • A crime is happening now or just happened
  • • You see a major crash or fire

What happens when you call: You'll be asked "Police, Fire, or Ambulance?" State which service you need. Give your location first, then explain the emergency. Stay calm and answer all questions.

🚗 Traffic Safety - *555

Call *555 from your mobile when:

  • • You see dangerous or drunk driving
  • • There's non-urgent road hazards (debris, broken traffic lights)
  • • You witness sustained bad driving that could cause a crash
  • • Traffic incidents that don't require immediate emergency response

Important: *555 is free from all mobile networks. Pull over safely before calling if you're driving. Have the vehicle registration, location, and direction of travel ready.

👮 NZ Police - Non-Emergency

For non-urgent matters, crimes that have already happened, or general inquiries.

  • Phone: 105 (non-emergency number)
  • Online Reporting: 105.police.govt.nz
  • Report Online: Police Online Forms
  • Website: www.police.govt.nz

Use 105 or online forms for: Reporting theft or property damage after it happened, lost property, providing information about crime, reporting historical incidents, or getting updates on reports you've made.

📝 When to Use Online Police Reporting

Online reporting is fastest for non-urgent matters. You'll get a reference number immediately.

  • Perfect for:
  • • Theft from vehicles (after the fact)
  • • Property damage/vandalism
  • • Lost property reports
  • • Theft in a public place
  • • Providing information about a crime

You'll need: Date and time of incident, location details, description of what happened, serial numbers or identifying features of stolen items, and any witness information.

⚠️ Important Safety Information

  • If in doubt, call 111 - It's better to be safe
  • Trust your instincts - If something feels wrong, get help
  • You won't get in trouble for calling emergency services in good faith
  • Language barriers: Interpreters are available - just state your language
  • Silent calls: If you can't speak, press 55 on your phone keypad when asked

Independent Dispute Resolution Options

🏛️ Disputes Tribunal

The Disputes Tribunal provides a quick, inexpensive, and informal way to resolve disputes up to $30,000. No lawyers are involved, and the filing fee is only $45.

  • Website: www.disputestribunal.govt.nz
  • Apply Online: File a Claim Online
  • Phone: 0800 367 7874 (0800 COURTS)
  • Cost: $45 for claims under $2,000; $90 for claims $2,000-$5,000

🚗 Motor Vehicle Disputes Tribunal

Specializes in disputes about motor vehicle sales and repairs. Can hear claims up to $100,000 for vehicle purchases.

  • Website: www.mvdt.govt.nz
  • Apply: Application Forms
  • Phone: 0800 367 6838
  • Email: mvdt@justice.govt.nz
  • Cost: $50-$200 depending on claim value

📞 Citizens Advice Bureau (CAB)

Free, confidential advice on your rights and obligations. They can help you understand your options and guide you through dispute processes.

  • Website: www.cab.org.nz
  • Phone: 0800 367 222
  • Find Local Office: Location Finder
  • Cost: FREE

⚖️ Community Law Centres

Free legal advice for people who can't afford a lawyer. They can help with consumer disputes, contracts, and understanding your rights.

  • Website: communitylaw.org.nz
  • Phone: 0800 155 529
  • Find Local Centre: Get Legal Help
  • Cost: FREE for eligible people

🛡️ Commerce Commission

Investigates breaches of consumer law including the Fair Trading Act and Consumer Guarantees Act. They don't resolve individual disputes but can take action against businesses.

  • Website: www.comcom.govt.nz
  • Make a Complaint: Online Complaint Form
  • Phone: 0800 943 600
  • Email: contact@comcom.govt.nz

🚦 NZ Transport Agency (NZTA)

For vehicle compliance, certification issues, and transport service complaints.

  • Website: www.nzta.govt.nz
  • Contact: Contact NZTA
  • Phone: 0800 108 809

💡 Before You File a Complaint

  • • Always try to resolve the issue directly with us first
  • • Keep all documentation, receipts, and correspondence
  • • Take photos if relevant to your complaint
  • • Note dates, times, and names of people you've dealt with
  • • Most disputes can be resolved without tribunal proceedings

How to Get the Best Results from Government Services

📋 Before Contacting Any Department

  1. Gather your documentation: Have all relevant paperwork, reference numbers, and correspondence ready
  2. Know your timeline: Write down dates of incidents, previous contacts, and deadlines
  3. Be specific: Know exactly what outcome you're seeking
  4. Try the right channel first: Many issues can be resolved online without waiting on hold
  5. Keep records: Note who you spoke to, when, and what was agreed

💡 Tips for Each Service

Disputes Tribunal:

  • File quickly - you have 6 years for contract disputes, 3 years for motor vehicle disputes
  • The $45 fee is worth it for disputes over $500
  • Bring 3 copies of all documents to your hearing
  • Decisions are final and enforceable - there's no appeal

Commerce Commission:

  • They look for patterns - your complaint helps even if they don't act immediately
  • Be factual, not emotional in your complaint
  • Include copies of advertisements or misleading claims
  • They prioritize cases affecting many consumers

Citizens Advice Bureau:

  • Visit in person if possible - they can help fill out forms
  • They're completely confidential and won't judge
  • Ask for referrals to specialist help if needed
  • They can explain legal letters and documents

Community Law:

  • Check eligibility online first - there are income thresholds
  • Book appointments early - they're often busy
  • Bring all documents to your first meeting
  • They can represent you in some tribunals

✅ Most Important Advice

  • • Act quickly: Many services have time limits for complaints
  • • Stay calm and factual: Emotional complaints are less effective
  • • Use online services: Often faster than phone calls
  • • Keep everything in writing: Email creates a paper trail
  • • Know your rights: Read the Consumer Guarantees Act summary on consumer.govt.nz
  • • Escalate appropriately: Start with the business, then try mediation, then tribunals
