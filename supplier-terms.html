Supplier Agreement

Effective Date: 1 April 2024

1. Definitions

1.1 Company: The business operations managed by Eek Mechanical Ltd ("Eek Mechanical"), as the parent entity, including all its worldwide subsidiaries and affiliates. The Company's rights and obligations under this Agreement extend to all entities operating under its control, ensuring compliance with the laws of New Zealand, applicable international laws, and regulations governing its global operations.

1.2 Supplier: The entity providing goods and/or services under this Agreement.

1.3 Goods: Products or materials supplied by the Supplier to the Company.

1.4 Services: Work or performance rendered by the Supplier.

1.5 Agreement: This document, including all referenced schedules, appendices, and policies.

1.6 Applicable Laws: All laws, regulations, and standards relevant to the Supplier's activities.

1.7 Force Majeure: Unforeseen events beyond reasonable control preventing performance.

1.8 IP Rights: Intellectual property rights, including copyrights, patents, trademarks, and trade secrets.

2. Becoming an Eek Mechanical Supplier

2.1 Pathways to Supplier Status: A Supplier becomes a participating member of the Eek Mechanical contractor network by either:

  • completing the supplier registration form at https://www.eek.nz/supplier; or
  • accepting any job or work order issued by Eek Mechanical, regardless of whether the formal registration process has been completed.

2.2 Acceptance of Terms: By taking either of the actions described in Clause 2.1, the Supplier confirms their acceptance of all terms and conditions contained in this Agreement, including any referenced policies or schedules. Acceptance of a job constitutes contractual agreement.

2.3 Network Participation and Public Representation: Suppliers acknowledge and agree that they are part of the Eek Mechanical network of mechanics and contractors—a global network delivering roadside assistance and related services on behalf of the Company. Eek Mechanical may publicly represent the Supplier's participation in this network in all customer communications, including but not limited to booking confirmations, progress updates, and marketing references. The Supplier consents to being identified by name, region, and trade classification for operational and commercial purposes.

2.4 Privacy Waiver and Data Disclosure: The Supplier grants Eek Mechanical a standing waiver to disclose necessary business and contact information to customers for the purpose of delivering services. This includes, but is not limited to, business name, dispatch contact number, service credentials, vehicle registration, technician name, and estimated arrival times. This waiver is granted under applicable data protection laws and remains in effect for the duration of the Supplier's participation in the network.

2.5 Voluntary Withdrawal from the Network: To exit the Eek Mechanical network, the Supplier must submit a formal request via the withdrawal form at https://www.eek.nz/supplier-relations. The request must clearly and explicitly state the intent to withdraw from the network. Informal notices, lack of responsiveness, or failure to use the prescribed form will not be accepted as valid withdrawal. Upon confirmation, the Supplier will be removed from the active supplier database and no longer receive job offers.

2.6 Documentation Requirements: Suppliers must provide all required documentation, including certifications, insurance, and evidence of compliance with applicable legal and environmental standards, as directed by the Company.

2.7 Approval and Onboarding: All applications and documents submitted will be reviewed by Eek Mechanical's procurement team. Once approved, the Supplier will receive an onboarding package outlining purchase order protocols, invoicing procedures, and communication standards.

2.8 Ongoing Compliance: Suppliers must comply with Eek Mechanical's Supplier Code of Conduct and maintain continuous adherence to all Company policies, safety expectations, and legal requirements.

2.9 Payment Contingency: No payment for services rendered shall be processed unless the Supplier has completed the registration process and provided all required documentation. Eek Mechanical reserves the right to withhold payment for unregistered or non-compliant suppliers.

3. Supplier Obligations

3.1 Compliance with Laws and Regulations: The Supplier must comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and standards related to the manufacture, labelling, transportation, importation, exportation, licensing, and certification of Goods and Services.

3.2 Quality Assurance: The Supplier shall ensure that all Goods and Services meet or exceed the quality, specifications, and performance standards outlined by the Company or described in Purchase Orders.

3.3 Ethical Conduct: The Supplier must conduct business in an ethical manner, adhering to the Company's Supplier Code of Conduct and avoiding any conflicts of interest.

3.4 Indemnity and Liability: All liability arising from the Goods and Services, including but not limited to defects, non-compliance, negligence, or breach of this Agreement, falls solely and directly on the Supplier. The Supplier agrees to indemnify and hold the Company harmless from any associated losses, damages, or expenses.

3.5 Delivery and Performance: The Supplier must deliver Goods and perform Services in a timely and professional manner, meeting the schedules and requirements set forth by the Company.

3.6 Insurance: The Supplier shall maintain at all times: (a) public liability insurance of not less than NZD $2,000,000 per occurrence; (b) professional indemnity insurance (where applicable) of not less than NZD $1,000,000; (c) motor vehicle insurance covering all vehicles used in providing Services. The Supplier shall provide certificates of currency upon request and shall notify the Company immediately if any policy is cancelled, lapses, or is materially changed. Failure to maintain required insurance shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement.

4. Delivery of Goods

4.1 Delivery Requirements: The Supplier must deliver Goods to the location specified in the Purchase Order by the agreed-upon delivery date. Delivery must include all required documentation, including but not limited to consignment slips, packing lists, and handling instructions.

4.2 Risk and Title: Risk of loss or damage to the Goods remains with the Supplier until the Goods are successfully delivered to and accepted by the Company. Title to the Goods passes to the Company only upon full payment.

4.3 Inspection and Acceptance: The Company reserves the right to inspect the Goods upon delivery. Acceptance of the Goods occurs only after the Company confirms compliance with the agreed specifications. If the Goods fail to meet specifications, the Company may reject them at the Supplier's expense.

4.4 No Partial Delivery: Partial deliveries are not permitted unless explicitly authorized in writing by the Company. Unauthorized partial deliveries may be refused and returned at the Supplier's cost.

4.5 Late Delivery: Time is of the essence. The Supplier is liable for any losses incurred by the Company as a result of late delivery unless a delay is due to Force Majeure.

5. Supply of Services

5.1 Performance Standards: The Supplier shall provide all Services with due care, skill, and diligence, using appropriately qualified and trained personnel, in accordance with Good Industry Practice and any specifications provided by the Company.

5.2 Timeliness: The Supplier must perform the Services in accordance with the timelines specified in the Agreement or the applicable Purchase Order. Failure to meet agreed timelines may result in liability for damages unless caused by a Force Majeure event.

5.3 Personnel Requirements: The Supplier shall ensure all personnel assigned to the Services are properly trained, qualified, and authorized to perform their duties. At the Company's request, the Supplier must replace any personnel deemed unsuitable for performance of the Services.

5.4 Compliance and Cooperation: The Supplier must comply with all Company policies, safety protocols, and site-specific requirements when performing Services at a Company-controlled location.

5.5 Corrective Actions: If Services do not meet the agreed standards, the Supplier must promptly re-perform the Services at its own expense until compliance is achieved.

6. Warranties

6.1 Supplier Warranties: The Supplier warrants that:

  • 6.1.1 All Goods supplied are free from defects in design, materials, and workmanship.
  • 6.1.2 All Goods and Services conform to the specifications, standards, and requirements outlined in the Agreement or Purchase Orders.
  • 6.1.3 The Goods are new, unused, and fit for their intended purpose.
  • 6.1.4 The Services will be performed with due skill, care, and diligence by appropriately qualified personnel.

6.2 Legal Compliance: The Supplier warrants that all Goods and Services comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards.

6.3 Warranty Period: The Supplier warrants all Goods and Services for a minimum period of 12 months from the date of acceptance by the Company, unless otherwise specified in the Agreement.

6.4 Defects and Remedies:

  • 6.4.1 Supplier's Responsibility: The Supplier is fully liable and responsible for ensuring that all Goods and Services provided under this Agreement are free from defects, non-conformities, or deficiencies.
  • 6.4.2 Remedial Actions: If defects or non-conformities are discovered within the warranty period, the Supplier must, at its own expense and without delay: (a) Repair or replace defective Goods; (b) Re-perform defective Services; (c) Reimburse the Company for any costs incurred due to the defects, including inspection, handling, and logistics costs.
  • 6.4.3 Extended Liability: The Supplier's liability extends beyond the warranty period if latent defects, fraud, or gross negligence are identified.

6.5 Customer Disputes:

  • 6.5.1 Supplier's Responsibility for Customer Issues: The Supplier acknowledges and accepts full and ongoing responsibility for resolving all disputes, claims, or issues raised by customers of Eek Mechanical relating to the Goods or Services provided under this Agreement.
  • 6.5.2 Resolution Obligations: The Supplier shall: (a) Address such disputes in a timely, professional, and satisfactory manner; (b) Indemnify and hold the Company harmless from any customer claims or damages arising from defective Goods or Services, including legal costs incurred in managing or defending such claims.
  • 6.5.3 No Recourse to the Company: The Supplier shall not direct customers back to the Company for resolution and remains the sole entity accountable for all outcomes.

6.6 Full Liability and No Limitation:

  • 6.6.1 Unlimited Supplier Responsibility: The Supplier's warranties and obligations under this Agreement shall not be limited or excluded by any provision of this Agreement or applicable law.
  • 6.6.2 Scope of Liability: The Supplier's liability encompasses all direct, indirect, and consequential damages, including but not limited to: (a) Losses caused by defective Goods or Services; (b) Business disruptions suffered by the Company or its customers; (c) Costs, expenses, and legal fees incurred as a result of the Supplier's non-compliance or failure.

7. Payment Terms

7.1 Invoice Requirements: Invoices must reference the relevant Purchase Order and provide clear details of the Goods or Services supplied.

7.2 Disputed Invoices: Disputed invoices may be withheld until resolution.

7.3 Reasonable Accounts Rendered: All amounts invoiced by the Supplier must be reasonable. Eek Mechanical shall retain the first $850 of any amount collected from the customer. The Supplier shall be entitled to payment only from amounts collected in excess of $850, up to a maximum of the amount invoiced by the Supplier. If the amount collected from the customer is $850 or less, the Supplier shall not be entitled to any payment from Eek Mechanical for that transaction, and the provisions of Clause 7.4 shall apply to any amount the Supplier claims remains owing. The Supplier acknowledges and agrees that Eek Mechanical is not obligated to pay any amount unless and until customer payment exceeds $850.

7.4 Unpaid Customer Debts: Any remaining unpaid debts from customers of Eek Mechanical, after applying the provisions of Clause 7.3, shall be assigned to the Supplier for collection. Upon such assignment, the Supplier assumes all rights and obligations to recover payment directly from the customer. The Supplier hereby consents to such assignment and agrees to execute any documents necessary to perfect the assignment. Until recovery, the debt remains the Supplier's responsibility and these unpaid amounts are no longer considered bills owed by Eek Mechanical. The Supplier assumes full responsibility for recovering payment directly from the customer.

7.5 Set-Off Rights: The Company may set off any amounts owed to the Supplier against: (a) any amounts the Supplier owes to the Company under this Agreement; (b) any damages, losses, or costs arising from the Supplier's breach; (c) any amounts the Company has paid or may become liable to pay as a result of the Supplier's acts or omissions; (d) any customer refunds, chargebacks, or credits attributable to the Supplier's work; (e) any amounts recoverable under Clause 9.5 (Disclosure-Induced Non-Payment). Set-off may be applied without prior notice and the Company's calculation shall be conclusive absent manifest error.

7.6 Payment Authority: The Supplier acknowledges and agrees that full authority over the timing and terms of payments is ceded to Eek Mechanical. The Company retains the exclusive right to determine payment schedules and modify them at its sole discretion, based on its financial and operational needs. While the Company endeavors to settle accounts promptly, it is under no obligation to adhere to supplier payment timelines. This clause supersedes and overrides any payment terms or conditions stipulated by the Supplier, regardless of any prior agreements, invoices, or customary practices.

7.7 Chargebacks and Refunds: If the Company incurs any chargeback, payment reversal, or is required to issue a refund to a customer in respect of Goods or Services provided by the Supplier, the Supplier shall be liable for the full amount of such chargeback or refund plus any associated fees, costs, or penalties imposed by payment processors. The Company may recover these amounts by set-off under Clause 7.5 or by invoice to the Supplier, payable within 7 days.

7.8 Currency: All amounts under this Agreement are in New Zealand Dollars (NZD) unless otherwise specified in writing.

8. Materials

8.1 Ownership of Materials: Any materials, data, or information provided by Eek Mechanical to the Supplier for the purpose of fulfilling this Agreement remain the exclusive property of the Company.

8.2 Permitted Use: The Supplier may only use such materials for the agreed purpose of fulfilling its obligations under this Agreement.

8.3 Return of Materials: Upon the Company's request or upon termination of this Agreement, the Supplier must promptly return all materials provided by the Company, including any copies or derivatives made during the course of this Agreement.

8.4 Safeguarding Materials: The Supplier shall ensure that all materials provided by the Company are safeguarded against unauthorized use, loss, or damage.

9. Confidentiality

9.1 Confidentiality Obligations: The Supplier agrees to keep all information, whether oral, written, or electronic, related to Eek Mechanical confidential. This includes, but is not limited to, operational, financial, strategic, and customer-related data.

9.2 Restriction on Financial Disclosures:

  • 9.2.1 The Supplier shall not disclose, discuss, or share any information about the financial terms of this Agreement, including but not limited to what the Company pays the Supplier, with the Company's customers, other suppliers, or any third parties.
  • 9.2.2 The Supplier is strictly prohibited from inquiring with the Company's customers about the amounts they have paid to Eek Mechanical.

9.3 Authorized Use: Confidential information may only be used by the Supplier to fulfil its obligations under this Agreement.

9.4 Breach of Confidentiality and Penalty:

  • 9.4.1 Any disclosure of information that Eek Mechanical determines, at its sole discretion, to be confidential, including violations of Clause 9.2, will constitute a material breach of this Agreement. The Supplier acknowledges that the determination of confidentiality rests entirely with the Company, and no correspondence or dispute regarding the classification of such information will be entered into. The Company reserves the right to take immediate legal or contractual action, including termination of this Agreement and pursuit of damages, as a result of any breach of confidentiality.
  • 9.4.2 In the event of such a breach, the Supplier shall forfeit any amounts currently outstanding and owed to them by the Company as a penalty. This penalty will apply without prejudice to any other remedies available to the Company, including termination of the Agreement and/or legal action.

9.5 Liability for Disclosure-Induced Non-Payment: If a Supplier's disclosure of financial information to a customer, or any communication by the Supplier that causes or contributes to a customer disputing, withholding, reducing, or refusing payment to Eek Mechanical, the Supplier shall be liable to the Company for the full amount invoiced to that customer. This liability arises immediately upon customer non-payment or underpayment and is in addition to (not in substitution for) any other remedies under this Agreement including forfeiture under Clause 9.4. The Supplier agrees that: (a) the Company's invoice amount to the customer represents the measure of damages; (b) this sum is a genuine pre-estimate of the Company's loss; (c) the Company need not prove that the Supplier's disclosure was the sole cause, only that it was a contributing factor; (d) the Supplier waives any right to dispute the quantum of damages under this clause.

9.6 Survival: The confidentiality obligations under this Clause 9 shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement indefinitely.

10. Intellectual Property

10.1 Ownership of Developments: Any intellectual property rights arising from or created in connection with the Supplier's provision of Goods or Services under this Agreement shall become the exclusive property of Eek Mechanical.

10.2 Pre-existing Intellectual Property: The Supplier retains ownership of any intellectual property that was developed independently of this Agreement. However, the Supplier grants Eek Mechanical a non-exclusive, royalty-free, perpetual license to use such pre-existing intellectual property as required to utilize the Goods or Services provided under this Agreement.

10.3 Assignment of Rights: The Supplier agrees to execute any documents or take any actions necessary to transfer ownership of intellectual property rights created under this Agreement to Eek Mechanical.

10.4 Third-party Intellectual Property: The Supplier warrants that all Goods and Services supplied do not infringe upon the intellectual property rights of any third party. The Supplier shall indemnify Eek Mechanical against any claims, damages, or expenses arising from such infringement.

10.5 Confidentiality of Intellectual Property: The Supplier shall treat all intellectual property and proprietary information related to the Company as confidential and subject to the confidentiality provisions in Clause 9 of this Agreement.

11. Non-Competition

11.1 Non-Competition Obligation: During the term of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years following its termination or expiration, the Supplier agrees not to directly or indirectly compete with Eek Mechanical in the provision of similar Goods or Services within the same geographic regions or markets in which the Company operates.

11.2 Prohibition on Solicitation:

  • 11.2.1 The Supplier shall not solicit, approach, or accept business from any customers, clients, or business partners of Eek Mechanical with whom the Supplier interacted, became aware of, or had dealings with during the term of this Agreement.
  • 11.2.2 This clause applies regardless of whether the solicitation or approach is for the Supplier's benefit or for the benefit of another entity.

11.3 Non-Circumvention: The Supplier agrees not to bypass Eek Mechanical by providing Goods or Services directly to the Company's customers without prior written consent from the Company.

11.4 Remedies for Breach: A breach of this non-competition clause shall entitle the Company to:

  • 11.4.1 Seek injunctive relief to prevent further violations.
  • 11.4.2 Claim damages, including but not limited to lost profits or revenues resulting from the breach.
  • 11.4.3 Terminate any remaining obligations under this Agreement and pursue other legal remedies.

11.5 Liquidated Damages for Circumvention: If the Supplier provides services directly to any customer of Eek Mechanical in breach of Clause 11.3, the Supplier shall pay to the Company, as liquidated damages and not as a penalty, a sum equal to three times the amount the customer would have been charged by Eek Mechanical for equivalent services, or NZD $5,000, whichever is greater. The Supplier acknowledges this represents a genuine pre-estimate of the Company's loss including damage to customer relationships and market position.

12. Privacy Compliance (New Zealand Privacy Act 2020)

12.1 Compliance with the Privacy Act 2020: The Supplier shall comply with all obligations under the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020, including adherence to the 13 Information Privacy Principles (IPPs) governing the collection, use, storage, and disclosure of personal information.

12.2 Collection of Personal Information:

  • 12.2.1 The Supplier must collect personal information only for lawful purposes directly related to the performance of this Agreement.
  • 12.2.2 The Supplier must ensure that individuals are informed about why their personal information is being collected and how it will be used, as required under the Privacy Act 2020.

12.3 Use and Disclosure:

  • 12.3.1 Personal information obtained under this Agreement must only be used for the purposes specified by the Company.
  • 12.3.2 The Supplier must not disclose personal information to third parties without the prior written consent of the Company, except as permitted by the Privacy Act 2020.

12.4 Storage and Security: The Supplier must take all reasonable steps to protect personal information from unauthorized access, misuse, loss, or disclosure, in accordance with the Privacy Act 2020.

12.5 Accuracy of Information: The Supplier must take reasonable steps to ensure that personal information is accurate, complete, and up to date before using or disclosing it.

12.6 Access and Correction: The Supplier acknowledges that individuals have the right to access and correct their personal information under the Privacy Act 2020. The Supplier shall cooperate with the Company to facilitate these rights.

12.7 Notification of Privacy Breaches: In the event of a privacy breach involving personal information provided under this Agreement, the Supplier must notify the Company immediately and assist in fulfilling any mandatory reporting obligations under the Privacy Act 2020.

12.8 Indemnity for Breach: The Supplier shall indemnify and hold the Company harmless against any losses, damages, fines, or penalties incurred as a result of the Supplier's failure to comply with its obligations under this Clause or the Privacy Act 2020.

18.2 Termination for Convenience: The Company may terminate this Agreement for any reason by providing the Supplier with 30 days' written notice.

18.3 Supplier's Right to Terminate: The Supplier may terminate this Agreement if the Company fails to make undisputed payments owed under this Agreement within 30 days of receiving written notice of non-payment.

18.4 Consequences of Termination:

  • 18.4.1 Upon termination, the Supplier must immediately cease using any materials, data, or confidential information provided by the Company and return or securely destroy all such items as directed by the Company.
