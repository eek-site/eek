Why Fuel Extraction is Better

1. Faster Service

  • Fuel extraction: 30-90 minutes on-site
  • Towing: Hours or days (towing time + workshop wait + service time)
  • Extraction gets you back on the road immediately

2. Lower Cost

  • Fuel extraction: $399-$849 (one service)
  • Towing: $200+ (towing) + $400+ (workshop service) = $600+ total
  • Extraction is typically cheaper overall

3. Less Disruption

  • Fuel extraction: Vehicle stays where it is, service completed on-site
  • Towing: Vehicle must be moved, you must arrange transport, significant disruption
  • Extraction minimizes disruption to your day

4. Immediate Solution

  • Fuel extraction: Problem fixed on-site, you drive away
  • Towing: Problem fixed later at workshop, you must return to collect vehicle
  • Extraction provides immediate resolution

When Towing Might Be Necessary

While fuel extraction is usually the better choice, towing may be necessary in rare cases:

  • Severe contamination: If extensive damage has already occurred
  • Workshop service required: If comprehensive workshop service is needed
  • Vehicle location: If vehicle is in an inaccessible location
  • Specialist equipment: If specialized workshop equipment is required

Important: In most cases, fuel extraction is sufficient and preferable. We'll assess your situation and recommend the best approach.
