Our Professional Assessment Process

1. Incident Verification & Documentation

We thoroughly document the incident including date, time, location, circumstances, and contamination details. Our reports explicitly confirm damage consistency with the reported event.

2. Comprehensive Damage Assessment

Every affected component is inspected and tested. We provide objective evidence of damage through:

  • Fuel system pressure testing
  • Injector spray pattern analysis
  • Electronic resistance testing
  • Visual contamination inspection

3. Technical Repair Justification

For each component, we explain:

  • What failed: Specific component and failure mechanism
  • Why it failed: Technical explanation (e.g., lubrication loss, metal-to-metal wear)
  • Test results: Objective measurements showing failure
  • Why replacement is necessary: Manufacturer protocols and industry standards

4. Cost Justification & Validation

We demonstrate cost reasonableness through three independent methods:

  • Necessity: Objective test failures prove component replacement required
  • Savings: Comparative analysis shows costs prevented by immediate action
  • Standards: Manufacturer and industry protocols require the work performed

5. Quality Assurance & Roadworthiness

Post-repair testing confirms all systems are functioning correctly and your vehicle is safe to drive.

Example: Petrol in Diesel Incident

Here's how our professional reporting prevents claim issues:

Damage Progression Timeline

Immediate Extraction (0-30 seconds): $400-$800 (extraction only)
Brief Operation (30-120 seconds): $1,500-$3,500 (pump + injectors likely)
Extended Operation (2-5 minutes): $5,000-$15,000+ (complete system failure, potential total loss)

Our Service: Immediate extraction performed — $800 cost, $4,200-$14,200 damage prevented

Technical Component Justification

Fuel Pump Replacement - $1,200

Why it failed: Diesel fuel provides essential lubrication to pump internals. Petrol contamination acts as a solvent, stripping lubrication and causing metal-to-metal contact, generating debris and pump seizure.

Test evidence: Fuel pressure test: 850 kPa (specification: 1,800-2,000 kPa) — FAILED (58% below minimum)

Why replacement required: Internal mechanical damage from metal-to-metal contact cannot be reversed through cleaning. Manufacturer service protocol requires replacement following contamination exposure with engine operation.
